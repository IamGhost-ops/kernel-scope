# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct 


## Our Pledge

We are members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free expirience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible
disability, ethnicity, sex characteristic, gender identity and expression, level of expirience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal apperance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orintation.


We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and health community.


## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that  contributes to a positive environment for our community include:

* Demonstrating empathy and kidness toward other people 
* Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
* Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
* Accepting responsibility and apologizing to those affected by our mistakes, and learning from experience
* Focusing on what is best not just for us  as individuals, but for the overall community



Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

* The use of sexualized language or imagery, and unwelcome sexual attention or advances
* Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
* Public or private harassment, includingpatronizing or condescending behavior towards project authors
* Publishing others' private information, such as a physical or email address, without their explicit permission
* Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inapprociate in a proffesional setting



## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers areresposible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take approciate, fair, and corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Project maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, questions, and other contributions that are not aligned to this Code Of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.



## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, and also applies when an individual is officialy representing the community in public spaces. Examples of representing our community include using an official e-mail addres, posting via an official social media account, or acting as an appointed representative at an online or offline event.



## Enforcement Guidelines

Project maintainers will follow these Community Impact Guidelines in determining theconsequences for any action they deem in violation of this Code of Conduct:



### 1.Correction

**Community Impact**: Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed  unprofessional or unwelcome in the community.
**Consequence**: A private or public written warning from project maintainers, providing clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the bahavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.



### 2.Warning

**Community Impact**: A violation through a single incident or series of actions.
**Consequence**: A warning with cosequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing  the Code of Conduct, for a specified period of time. This includes avoiding interactions in community spaces as well as external channels like social media. Violating these terms may lead to a permament ban.



### 3.Ban
   
**Community Impact**: A patern of violation of community standards, including substained harassing behavior, or demonstration of serious disregard for the community or its creators.
**Consequence**: A permament ban from any sort of public interacyion within the community project, including immediate blocking of the user account.
